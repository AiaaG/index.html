Dear mother,
A knight undercover.
Agony crusher,
My protector.
Queen mother,
I love you, dear.
I wish that your happiness,
Never disappears.






He'd been the center of my life.
And as he stabbed me,
I saw his eyes.
Calm, Atlantic,
And young, somehow.
Though no longer immature,
no longer demure,
no longer pure.
I wonder what had occured?
who altered his innocent soul?
What made my delicate, debonair, dearest Dorian
turn into a
Damned, decadent, dreadful man?
Tears streamed down my cheek,
yearning for those days when he was him,
and the scent of roses was still around.

"Adieu, Horatio,"
He said, with a bitter smile on his face.
"Adieu, my lord."
I replied, with trembling hands and a pale face.
I gripped my fist with complete sorrow,
How could these bloodthirsty, monstrous,
Damned Danes
Destroy the life of my good sweet lord?
Yet nobody seems to even care,
as if it was a normal affair.
When I sensed his ladt breath,
My heart and sould shattered.
I have nothing now, and am prepared to perish,
Yet I know I shall not.
So I stand;wipe my tears.
And go to fulfil his last longings.
Sleep well, my lord;
And I shall take care of Denmark.
